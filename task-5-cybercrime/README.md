**Case Study: Cyber Crime – UPI Fraud**

Unified Payments Interface (UPI) fraud is a rapidly growing cyber crime in which criminals trick users into transferring money through digital payment apps such as Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm. This type of fraud exploits users’ lack of awareness about how UPI transactions work and relies heavily on social engineering techniques.

Typically, the fraud begins when the scammer contacts the victim through a phone call, SMS, social media message, or a fake customer care number found online. The criminal pretends to be a bank official, delivery agent, buyer from an online marketplace, or technical support executive. In the next step, the fraudster convinces the victim that they need to “verify” their account, receive a payment, or claim a refund. The victim is then asked to approve a “collect request” sent through a UPI app or to share a One-Time Password (OTP) or UPI PIN. Once the victim authorizes the request or shares sensitive details, the money is instantly transferred from the victim’s bank account to the scammer’s account. Because UPI transactions are real-time, the funds are often difficult to recover.

The people most commonly targeted include students, elderly individuals, small business owners, and users who are new to digital payments. Individuals searching for customer support numbers online are also frequent victims, as scammers create fake listings.

The consequences of UPI fraud include financial loss, stress, and reduced trust in digital payment systems. In some cases, victims may also experience identity theft if personal information is stolen. This case highlights the importance of digital literacy, verifying official sources, and never sharing UPI PINs or approving unknown payment requests.
